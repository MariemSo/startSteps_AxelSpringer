Where we come from

Germany

Fun Fact

In Germany, there's a popular tradition called "Kaffeeklatsch," which is essentially a coffee gossip session. Friends and family gather in the afternoon to drink coffee, eat cake, and chat about everything under the sun. It's like a mini party dedicated to coffee, cake, and catching up on all the juicy details of life. Think of it as the German version of a tea party, but with more cake and perhaps a bit more gossip!

Brazil

Fun Fact

In Brazil, there's a tradition during the New Year where people wear colored underwear to attract good fortune. Different colors symbolize different kinds of luck: red is for love, yellow is for wealth, and white is for peace. So, don't be surprised if you see Brazilians shopping for colorful undies as part of their New Year preparations!

Hungary

Fun Fact

In Hungary, it's considered good luck to sit on a baby before its first birthday. This unusual custom is believed to ensure that the baby grows up strong and healthy. So, don't be surprised if you see a Hungarian family gently placing a child on a relative's lap—they're just passing on some good luck!

France

Fun Fact

In France, there's a village named "Y" which is the shortest place name in the country. The residents of Y are humorously referred to as "Ypsiloniens," and their village sign is a popular spot for tourists to stop and take photos, often pretending to be puzzled by the name.

kosovo

Fun Fact

In Kosovo, there is a humorous tradition called "pushing the car to the wedding." It's customary in some rural areas for the groom and his friends to pretend that the wedding car has broken down just before reaching the bride's house. They push the car a short distance as a playful stunt, symbolizing that the groom will overcome any obstacle to marry his bride. This often leads to a lot of laughter and is a light-hearted start to the wedding festivities!

Poland

Fun Fact

In Poland, there's a tradition called "Smigus-Dyngus" or "Wet Monday." It takes place on Easter Monday, where people playfully drench each other with water. It's not uncommon for friends, family, and even strangers to participate in this water fight, using water guns, buckets, or simply splashing water on each other. Legend has it that girls who get soaked will marry within the year, so it's all in good fun with a sprinkle of superstition!

Venezuela

Fun Fact

In Venezuela, there's a superstition that if you sweep someone's feet with a broom, it means they'll never get married. So, if you accidentally sweep someone's feet while cleaning, you might see them quickly doing a little dance or tapping their feet to shake off the bad luck and ensure their marital prospects remain intact!

Macedonia

Fun Fact

Macedonians have a playful way of celebrating birthdays. When someone's birthday comes around, it's a tradition for friends and family to sneak into the birthday person's home while they're sleeping and cover them in flour! This surprise wake-up call ensures that the birthday person starts their special day with a laugh, albeit a bit messy. It's all in good fun and adds an extra element of excitement to the birthday festivities!

New Zealand

Fun Fact

In New Zealand, there's a quirky tradition called "gumboot throwing." Originally started as a rural sport, it involves tossing a rubber boot (gumboot) as far as possible. It has since become a fun activity at festivals and events across the country, with competitions held to see who can throw their gumboot the farthest. It's a lighthearted way for Kiwis to showcase their strength and accuracy while enjoying some good-natured competition!

Tunisia

Fun Fact

In Tunisia, it's not unusual to see people drinking tea... with pine nuts! This local custom involves adding pine nuts to a glass of sweet, minty tea. The pine nuts float on top, and as you sip the tea, you nibble on the nuts. The combination might seem unusual to outsiders, but Tunisians enjoy this quirky and tasty twist on a traditional beverage. It's both a snack and a drink in one!
